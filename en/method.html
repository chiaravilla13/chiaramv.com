Method

A 5-step method to create clarity before building.

A guided path to turn context, value and goals into direction, messaging, system and coherent touchpoints.

How I work

Each step is designed to reduce ambiguity and increase coherence: first we understand the problem, then we decide what leads, then we translate it into system and application.

AI can help make some steps faster or smoother. But sounding better, on its own, is not enough: first you need direction, criteria and priorities.

Understanding and context

We bring goals and scenario into focus: market, competitors, people, priorities.

Direction and choices

We turn insights into direction and messaging: clear decisions, not arbitrary ones.

System and guidelines

We build a repeatable system: identity, rules and materials to work with confidence and consistency.

The 5 steps

The path can adapt to where you are now, but the logic stays the same: direction first, then construction.

Analysis & research

Sector, competitors, alternatives, people. Understanding the context to identify real opportunities.

  • Competitor and alternatives snapshot
  • Target and needs hypotheses
  • Opportunities and constraints (what to avoid)

Strategic direction

We define what the brand stands for and why it should be chosen: proposition, differences, messaging.

  • Proposition and proof
  • Key messages + “what not to say”
  • Priorities (guided choices)

Brand identity

Values, personality and promise: the base for recognisable communication.

  • Personality and principles
  • Promise and tone (editorial direction)
  • Alignment between offer and perception

Brandbook

Clear rules to use the brand well, every time: a visual and applied system.

  • Palette, typography, graphic elements
  • Usage rules and examples
  • Assets and templates, if included

Communication strategy

Messages, tone, channels and content: a coherent system to communicate more effectively.

  • Pillars and editorial series
  • Priority touchpoints and channels
  • Action plan (what to do first)

What you get

  • Grounded decisions, not taste-based choices
  • A clear, defensible direction
  • A repeatable identity
  • Recognisable messaging and tone
  • Guidelines and materials ready to use
